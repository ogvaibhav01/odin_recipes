Paneer Tikka

discription

Paneer Tikka is a popular Indian appetizer made from marinated paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cubes that are grilled or baked to perfection. The paneer is typically marinated in a mixture of yogurt and spices, which gives it a flavorful and tangy taste. The dish is often served with mint chutney and is enjoyed for its smoky flavor and soft texture.

Ingredients

  • 250 grams paneer (cubed)
  • 1/2 cup thick yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Vegetables for skewering (bell peppers, onions, tomatoes)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, cumin powder, lemon juice, salt, and oil to make the marinade.
  2. Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and coat them well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Preheat your grill or oven to 200°C (392°F).
  4. Thread the marinated paneer cubes and vegetables onto skewers.
  5. Place the skewers on a baking tray lined with foil or directly on the grill.
  6. Grill or bake for 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally, until the paneer is cooked and slightly charred.
  7. Serve hot with mint chutney and lemon wedges.
