Daal Chawal

Description

Daal Chawal is a traditional Indian dish that consists of lentils (daal) served with rice (chawal). It is a staple meal in many Indian households and is known for its simplicity, nutritional value, and comforting flavors. The lentils are typically cooked with spices, onions, tomatoes, and garlic to create a flavorful and hearty dish. Daal Chawal is often enjoyed with a side of pickles, yogurt, or salad.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup lentils (toor dal, moong dal, or masoor dal)
  • 1 cup rice (basmati or any long-grain rice)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 medium tomato (finely chopped)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Water (as needed)

Steps

  1. Rinse the lentils thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear. Soak them in water for about 30 minutes to an hour.
  2. Rinse the rice under running water until the water runs clear. Soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  3. In a large pot, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  6. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  7. Add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  8. Drain the soaked lentils and add them to the pot. Stir to coat the lentils with the spice mixture.
  9. Add about 3 cups of water (adjust based on the type of lentils used) and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer until the lentils are cooked and soft (about 20-30 minutes). You may need to add more water if it gets too thick.
  10. While the lentils are cooking, drain the soaked rice. In a separate pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add the rice and a pinch of salt. Cook until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed (about 15-20 minutes). Fluff the rice with a fork once done.
  11. Once the lentils are cooked, check the consistency. If it's too thick, you can add a little water to reach your desired consistency. Adjust the seasoning if needed.
  12. Garnish the daal with fresh coriander leaves.
  13. Serve the hot daal with steamed rice. Enjoy your comforting meal of Daal Chawal!
